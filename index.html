Subordinating Conjunctions


Today we mainly focussed on the following:

  • Subordinating conjunctions used in the following seven cases: comparison/contrast, condition, concession, time, place, manner and reason
  • Acronym “ON A WHITE BUS” for easy remembrance of subordinating conjunctions
  • Punctuation rules rules for using subordinating conjunctions

Types of Subordinating Conjunctions Examples
1. Subordinating Conjunctions for Comparison Rather than, Whereas, Whether, As much As, Than
2. Subordinating Conjunctions for Concession Although, Though, Even Though, Despite
3. Subordinating Conjunctions for Condition Only if, If, Unless, Even if, Lest, In case, Assuming that, and Provided that.
4. Subordinating Conjunctions for Time As soon as, As long as, Before, After, By the time, Once, Since, Now that, Until, Till, While, Whenever, When
5. Subordinating Conjunctions for Place Where and Wherever
6. Subordinating Conjunctions for Manner As though, How, As if
7. Subordinating Conjunctions for Reason Since, Because, So that, That, In order, As

Word meaning:
Venture: To go somewhere or do something that involves risk or uncertainty.
Use: Our venture into the deep forests of the Amazon was very thrilling.
Scavenge: search for and collect (anything usable) from discarded waste.
Use: Don't scavenge inside garbage bags for your expired vitamin pills.
Nibble: to eat or chew in small bits
Use: You should rather eat fast than nibble your food and miss the school bus.
